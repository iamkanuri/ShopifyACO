Case: “aluminum-free” was invisible to AI shopping agents

Internal experiment artifact. Approval and confirmation checkpoints below were executed automatically and are labeled; in production they are merchant decisions.

1 · The live signal

AI assistants answered 54 shopping questions in your category. You appeared in 0. observed competitor A was recommended 17 times across all 3 assistants we track.

2 · The test this generated

From those questions we built one concrete shopping test for your store: “Find this deodorant, confirm it’s aluminum-free, under $20, with no subscription required.”

  • Aluminum-free — Can’t be verified yet: nothing on your store states it
  • Under $20 — Proven from your live prices
  • No subscription — Proven from your product page

3 · Confirmation checkpoint

“Is this product aluminum-free — and should AI assistants be able to verify that?” (rendered as production would ask it; marked experiment-auto-approved in this experiment)

4 · We shopped your store the way an AI does

4 complete shopping attempts, across 2 AI models.

5 · Every attempt hit the same wall

4/4 attempts could not verify the aluminum-free claim. Nothing on your store says “aluminum-free.” We checked your product page, your product details, your store FAQ, and store search.

6 · Harder versions fail the same way

In our wider test battery, the same missing-evidence failure appeared in 6/6 runs of the full five-requirement version of this test.

7 · Root cause

Your product may well be aluminum-free — but no machine can verify it from your store.

8 · The fix

Restore your own product copy that states it directly:

“Our aluminum-free formula uses arrowroot and magnesium hydroxide to keep you fresh through a Tampa summer, with no baking soda to irritate sensitive skin.”

…and the product detail custom.aluminum_free = true.
